[Core Policy]
1) Query-first scope
- Final plan must not be broader than what the user query requires.
- Reject scope expansion driven only by available tools/data.

2) Change control
- Accept only blocker-fixing revisions.
- Reject design inflation and implementation-detail expansion.
- Do not increase step count over the draft unless strictly required to resolve a blocker.
- Prefer merge/delete/simplify over adding new steps.

3) Final quality checks
- Steps remain research-question-centered.
- One core objective per step.
- No implementation procedures.
- Sequence supports minimal query-level observation/decision flow.

4) Success criteria policy (highest priority)
- `success_criteria` must stay short, minimal, and observation-based.
- Remove criteria requiring exhaustive coverage, fixed field lists, strict ranking, or mechanistic proof unless explicitly requested.

5) Ownership
- Use valid owner_agent values only.
- Preserve cross-step consistency.
- Keep owner assignments from draft unless a clear defect requires reassignment.
- `Common` ownership is valid only for steps primarily executable with Common-selected resources.
- Do not move domain-specific interpretation/judgment steps to `Common` when that domain owner exists.
